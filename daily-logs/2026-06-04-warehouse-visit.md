## What I Learned

### 1. **Three-Stage Parcel Processing System**

The warehouse operates through three main functions:

#### **Stage 1: Inbound** 📬
- Incoming parcels are **received and scanned**
- Uses **DWS System** (Dimension, Weight, Scanning)
- Warehouse E has **22 DWS slots** for high-volume processing
- Parcels move through **automated conveyor system**
- Sorted by **destination zones**

#### **Stage 2: Auto Packing** 📦
- Parcels in sacks are **opened and rescanned**
- Secondary check ensures **operational accuracy**
- Parcels returned to conveyor
- Automatically sorted into **destination sacks by province**

#### **Stage 3: Outbound** 📤
- Final sorting and **loading for transportation**
- Staff ensure correct **destination routing**
- Efficient loading for **network distribution**

---

## Special Handling Processes

### **Reverse Conveyor System** 🔄
- **Problem:** Parcels with unclear or unreadable labels
- **Solution:** Auto-diverts to reverse conveyor line
- **Action:** Staff manually inspect and redirect
- **Outcome:** Maintains sorting accuracy, prevents misrouting

### **Lightweight Parcel System** ⚖️
- **Challenge:** Too light for automated sorting
- **Process:** Placed in larger bags until sufficient weight
- **Transfer:** To manual sorting team

### **Zip Tray System** 🛒
- **Problem:** Round/cylindrical parcels may roll
- **Solution:** Specialized conveyor for rolling items
- **Benefit:** Reduces operational disruptions
