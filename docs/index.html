SPK Gateway · Daily Learning Logs, 4–11 June 2026 · 基于 2026 年 6 月 4–11 日每日学习记录

What Is a Gateway?

A Gateway (GW) is a distribution center that consolidates and sorts parcels from branches and other gateways nationwide. Every parcel must pass through at least one gateway before reaching its destination. The network currently comprises 11 gateways and 6 hubs, with SPK (Samut Prakan) serving as the headquarters gateway.

Core Workflow — Three Stages

1. Inbound

Verify the work-assignment sheet (driver name / license plate / three security straps) → register in the queue and release the truck into a loading bay → scan to open the container → load the conveyor onto the truck → every parcel passes through a DWS machine (automated dimension–weight–scanning; 22 units operating simultaneously) → automatic sorting by destination zone

Checkpoint at the conveyor entrance: parcels whose three dimensions total more than 100 cm, or that weigh more than 50 kg, are classified as oversize and diverted to the Manual Team. VIP brands (Oppo, Vivo, etc.) must not enter the machines. Labels must face up, and parcels must be spaced at least 10 cm apart.

2. Auto Packing (“Sack House”)

Sacks arriving from sub-branches are opened, and individual parcels are fed onto the conveyor to be re-sorted by destination province. The sack house consists of four rings (A/B/C/D, equipped with 24/24/24/36 machines respectively) and accepts only parcels weighing ≤ 3 kg with combined dimensions ≤ 90 cm. Parcels whose combined dimensions exceed 180 cm cannot be placed in sacks.

Every special case has a defined path: round or rollable items go to the Flip Tray line; items that are too light are collected into a sack and sent to the Manual Team; parcels with unreadable labels circulate on the conveyor via the Reverse function until a staff member reads the label and pushes the parcel into the correct lane — if it still cannot be identified, it is sent to the end-of-line area.

3. Outbound

The main conveyor branches into province-specific lanes, where assigned staff pull parcels into their own lanes → every parcel is scanned via PDA before loading → loading arrangement: heavy items at the bottom; fragile items, VVIP parcels, and fruit at the rear in separate zones → truck departure

This sorting step is performed manually by design: automation was trialed, but the machines could not keep up with the volume, parcels were dropped, and SLA targets were missed. The pre-loading PDA scan also serves as proof that responsibility has been transferred away from the warehouse.

Supporting Systems

JMS + PDA

Every checkpoint scan creates a timestamp tagged with the responsible branch, so any lost or damaged parcel can be traced immediately to the party accountable.

Quality Control (QC)

Five units — Call Center / CCTV / Data / Packing / Store — oversee five KPIs:

≥ 99.2%On-time inbound / outbound
< 800 pcs/dayMis-sorted parcels
< 0.4%Missed scans
< 1%Warehouse clearance
MonitoredTransit delays

Damaged parcels follow a repacking process together with a traceback investigation to determine which party is at fault.

Call Center 1470

All calls first reach headquarters → the parcel’s tracking status is checked → the call is transferred to the location where the parcel currently is. If that location’s lines are full, the HQ call center takes the case and forwards it through the back-office system. The Gateway handles only three task types. For tracking cases where a parcel’s status appears stuck at the Gateway, the Gateway first gathers CCTV evidence to prove the parcel has already been dispatched; once cleared, responsibility shifts to the destination branch, which the Gateway then contacts directly. 1470/HQ holds the highest authority and may contact any level. No-label parcels: stored for 14 days; claimable 5 days after being registered as stranded; claims must be filed within 30 days of shipment.

This Week’s Takeaways

  1. Automation is not the answer everywhere. Outbound trialed machines, which could not keep up with the volume and caused SLA failures, so the step was returned to manual operation. The choice between people and machines must be made from real conditions on the ground — not from the assumption that automation is always superior.
  2. Every scan is evidence of accountability, not merely a tracking record. The system is designed so that the responsible party can be traced at every point, from the origin branch to the destination.
  3. A good system gives every edge case a way out (round items → Flip Tray; unreadable labels → the Reverse function; oversize items → Manual Team), leaving no parcel stranded without an owner.
  4. Clearly defined scopes of work are deliberate design. Each unit owns its own duties, keeping accountability clear and every step auditable, while coordination remains agile — when a parcel’s status appears stuck at the Gateway, the Gateway uses CCTV evidence to prove the parcel has been dispatched, then contacts the branch directly to follow up.

Suggestions

  1. Call Center 1470. The actual dropped-call rate (50–60%) is far from the target (≤ 3%). The bottleneck should be analyzed — number of lines, destination staffing, or peak hours — and the HQ fallback capacity expanded accordingly.
  2. Timely-scanning discipline. The lag between system status and physical reality (parcels that have arrived but are not yet scanned) misroutes calls and callback numbers, sending customers to the wrong region. Reducing missed scans at this single point would improve both KPIs and the customer experience at once.
  3. A standard central glossary. Equipment and system names differ across departments (e.g., Flip Tray, the Reverse function, JMS). A standardized glossary for new employees and trainees would reduce misunderstandings and make knowledge transfer more accurate.
  4. Conveyor spare-parts management — a safety concern. Several conveyor points are faulty. On-ground operators have duly reported them, and technicians stand ready to repair, but some parts are not stocked locally and cannot be sourced in Thailand; they must be ordered from China, which takes considerable time. Faulty points therefore remain unrepaired even though every party has done its job.

    Critically, some of these faulty points pose a direct safety risk to front-line staff, so the matter deserves urgent priority. Proposed actions: (1) assess and cordon off, or limit the use of, hazardous points while parts are awaited; (2) keep critical spare parts in stock locally, or schedule advance purchasing based on component life cycles.

    Note: resolving this is not merely a cost but an investment with direct returns — machinery returns to full capacity sooner, work flows out faster, backlogs shrink, and both the safety and the productivity of the Gateway improve at the same time.

什么是分拨中心（Gateway）？

Gateway（GW，分拨中心）负责汇集并分拣来自全国各网点及其他分拨中心的包裹——每件包裹在送达目的地之前，必须至少经过一个分拨中心。目前全网共有 11 个 GW 和 6 个 HUB，其中 SPK（北榄府）为总部直属分拨中心。

核心作业流程——三个阶段

一、进港（Inbound）

核对任务单（司机姓名／车牌／三道封条）→ 排队登记，放行车辆进入装卸口 → 扫描开柜 → 将传送带架上车厢 → 所有包裹经过 DWS 设备（自动称重、测量、扫描，共 22 台同时运行）→ 按目的区域自动分拣

传送带入口检查点：三边之和超过 100 厘米或重量超过 50 公斤的包裹属于超规件（Oversize），直接转入人工分拣组；VIP 品牌（Oppo、Vivo 等）禁止上机；面单不得朝下，包裹间距须保持 10 厘米以上。

二、自动装包（Auto Packing／“装包区”）

来自下级网点的袋装包裹拆袋后，逐件送上传送带，按目的省份重新分拣。装包区共设四个环线（A／B／C／D，分别配备 24／24／24／36 台设备），仅接收重量 ≤3 公斤、三边之和 ≤90 厘米的包裹；三边之和超过 180 厘米的包裹不得装袋。

所有特殊情况均有明确的处理路径：圆形或易滚动的包裹进入 Flip Tray（翻盘分拣线）；过轻的包裹集中装袋后转交人工组；面单无法识别的包裹通过 Reverse 功能在传送带上循环，由员工人工辨认面单并拨入正确通道；若仍无法识别，则送至线尾处理区。

三、出港（Outbound）

主传送带按省份分出支线，专人将包裹拉入各自负责的通道 → 装车前每件包裹须经 PDA 扫描 → 装车规范：重物在下；易碎品、VVIP 件及水果置于车尾并分区摆放 → 发车

该分拣环节特意采用人工操作：此前曾试用机器，但因瞬时件量过大、包裹掉落，SLA 无法达标，故改回人工。装车前的 PDA 扫描同时是责任移交的凭证——此后包裹若丢失，责任不再属于本仓。

支持系统

JMS + PDA

每个节点的扫描都会生成带网点标识的时间戳；包裹一旦丢失或破损，可立即追溯到责任方。

质控部门（QC）

下设五个单元——呼叫中心／CCTV／数据／包装／仓储——管控五项 KPI：

< 800 件/天错分拣
< 0.4%落扫
< 1%清场
持续监控中转超时

破损包裹按重新包装流程处理，并进行责任倒查，认定过错方。

呼叫中心 1470

所有来电先接入总部 → 查询包裹轨迹 → 将电话转接至包裹当前所在节点；若该节点坐席占线，则由总部呼叫中心受理，并通过后台系统转交跟进。分拨中心仅负责三类工单。当客户查件显示包裹状态滞留在分拨中心时，分拨中心会先调取 CCTV 证据，证明包裹已发出；责任确认转移至网点后，分拨中心可直接联系网点跟进。1470／总部拥有最高权限，可联系任何层级。无面单包裹：入库保存 14 天；登记滞留 5 天后可申请理赔；理赔须自寄件之日起 30 天内提出。

本周心得

  1. 自动化并非处处适用。出港环节曾试用机器，但因无法承受瞬时件量、导致 SLA 不达标而改回人工。用人还是用机器，应基于现场实际情况判断，而非默认“自动化一定更好”。
  2. 每一次扫描都是责任凭证，而不仅是轨迹记录。系统的设计使包裹在从始发网点到目的地的每个节点都能追溯到责任方。
  3. 好的系统让每一种特殊情况都有出路（圆形件 → Flip Tray；面单无法识别 → Reverse 功能；超规件 → 人工组），不让任何包裹处于无人负责的状态。
  4. 清晰的职责边界是有意为之的制度设计。各单位各司其职，责任明确、全程可审计，同时协作依然高效——当包裹状态滞留在分拨中心时，分拨中心先调取 CCTV 证据证明包裹已发出，随后即可直接联系网点跟进。

改进建议

  1. 呼叫中心 1470。实际掉线率（50–60%）与目标（≤3%）差距巨大，应分析瓶颈所在——坐席数量、目的节点人力或高峰时段——并相应扩充总部兜底坐席的承接能力。
  2. 及时扫描的执行纪律。系统状态与实际位置存在延迟（包裹已到达但尚未扫描），导致来电及回拨号码被错误路由、客户联系到错误的区域。仅改善这一个环节，即可同时提升 KPI 与客户体验。
  3. 建立统一的标准术语表。各部门对设备／系统的称呼不一致（如 Flip Tray、Reverse 功能、JMS），应为新员工和管培生编制标准术语文档，减少理解偏差，使知识传承更准确。
  4. 传送带备件管理——涉及安全问题。多处传送带存在故障，现场操作人员已按流程报修，技术人员也随时可以维修，但部分备件本地无库存且无法在泰国采购，须从中国订购，周期较长——尽管各方均已尽责，故障点仍长期未能修复。

    更重要的是，部分故障点对一线员工的人身安全构成直接威胁，应列为最高优先级。建议：（1）在等待备件期间，评估并隔离或限制使用存在危险的故障点；（2）在本地储备关键备件（critical spare parts），或按部件寿命周期提前规划采购。

    说明：解决此问题不仅是一项成本，更是一项回报直接的投资——设备更快恢复满负荷运转，出货更快，机前积压减少，分拨中心的安全水平与生产效率（productivity）同步提升。
